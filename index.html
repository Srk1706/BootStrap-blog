My Favorite Biryani List

Dindigul Biryani

Origin: Dindigul, Tamil Nadu

Cooked with basmati rice, usually mutton or chicken, and a blend of aromatic spices like cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and black pepper. Dindigul biryani is known for its spicy flavor and the use of tamarind or tomato paste to add a tangy touch.

Hyderabadi Biryani

Origin: Hyderabad, Telangana

Cooked with long-grain basmati rice, usually mutton or chicken, and a blend of aromatic spices like cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and saffron. The biryani is often layered with fried onions and topped with a flavorful gravy.

Lucknowi Biryani

Origin: Lucknow, Uttar Prades

Cooked with long-grain basmati rice,usually mutton or chicken,and a blend of aromatic spices like cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and saffron.The biryani is often layered with fried onions and topped with a flavorful gravy.

Kolkata Biryani

Origin: Kolkata, West Bengal

Cooked with basmati rice, usually mutton or chicken, and a blend of aromatic spices like cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and saffron. The biryani is often layered with fried onions and topped with a flavorful gravy.

Ambur Biryani

Origin: Ambur, Tamil Nadu

Cooked with Seeraga Samba rice, usually mutton, and a blend of aromatic spices like cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and black pepper. The biryani is often served with raita, onion salan, and a chutney.

Thalapakatti Biryani

Origin: Madurai, Tamil Nadu

Cooked with Seeraga Samba rice, usually mutton,a blend spices like cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and black pepper.The biryani is known for its rich and flavorful gravy, often as slightly sweet and tangy.
